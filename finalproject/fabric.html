Fabric

Flannel

noun
a kind of soft-woven fabric, typically made of wool or cotton and slightly milled and raised

Gingham

noun
lightweight plain-woven cotton cloth, typically checked in white and a bold color

Houndstooth

noun
a large checked pattern with notched corners suggestive of a canine tooth, typically used in cloth for jackets and suits

Jean

noun
heavy twilled cotton cloth, especially denim

Jersey

noun
a soft, fine knitted fabric

Lace

noun
a fine open fabric, typically one of cotton or silk, made by looping, twisting, or knitting thread in patterns and used especially for trimming garments

Leather

noun
a material made from the skin of an animal by tanning or a similar

Mohair

noun
a yarn or fabric made from mohair, typically mixed with wool

Moleskin

noun
a thick, strong cotton fabric with a shaved pile surface

Suede

noun
leather with the flesh side rubbed to make a velvety nap

Nylon

noun
fabric or yarn made from nylon fibers

Oxford

noun
a heavy cotton cloth chiefly used to make shirts

Satin

noun
a smooth, glossy fabric, usually of silk, produced by a weave in which the threads of the warp are caught and looped by the weft only at certain intervals

Polyester

noun
a fabric made from polyester fiber

Silk

noun
thread or fabric made from the fibre produced by the silkworm

Tulle

noun
a soft, fine silk, cotton, or nylon material like net, used for making veils and dresses

Velvet

noun
a closely woven fabric of silk, cotton, or nylon, that has a thick short pile on one side
