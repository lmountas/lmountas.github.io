About

My name is Lamia Mountassir, and I am currently a student at George Mason University studying English with a concentration in writing & rhetoric. By following this path, I have learned to write and create material for various audiences and situations. For example, I have written and done research to create content such as how-to videos and white papers. More specifically, I created a how-to video on how Mason students can create an email signature as well as a white paper on how health experts and scientists on Youtube can address concerns regarding COVID to the public.

During the Spring 2021 semester, I have had the opportunity to work on a collaboration project between GMU and Calvert County, MD, called Calvert County: Agriculture Past and Present. Throughout this project, I had the opportunity to engage with a farmer about the rural community, her experiences as a woman in agriculture, and environmental policies. Furthermore, I also learned how to interview and conducted a 1-hour phone interview as well as created a transcript, podcast, and log to supplement the interview. Before I graduate in December of 2021, I'm currently seeking to begin gaining work experience through opportunities in writing professionally within editorial, journalism, and digital media internships.

How-To Video Project

The How-To video project required creating a video that instructs an audience on a subject that is relevant within an academic or professional environment. For my video, I provide instructions for George Mason students on how to create an email signature within their MasonLive Outlook email.

White Paper & Infographic Project

In my White Paper & Infographic Project, I focus on persuading health experts and scientists on Youtube to use the communication tactics I outline when encouraging the public to follow COVID safety guidelines and have their videos better reach and resonate with audiences during the pandemic.

Calvert County: Past & Present Agriculture Podcast

The final presentation for the project required creating a 5-minute podcast that reflects on the content of the 1-hour interview on agriculture in Calvert County. More specifically, the audio introduces the subject of the interview, Susie Hance-Wells, and conveys the sentiments she emphasized concerning the current policies and restrictions on those in the agricultural field and the importance of supporting those in the occupation.
