How-To Video Project

The How-To video project required creating a video that instructs an audience on a subject that is relevant within an academic or professional environment. For my video, I provide instructions for George Mason students on how to create an email signature within their MasonLive Outlook email.

Through completing this project,it enabled me to use the many skills I've acquired over the years, including my video editing and video creation abilities, as well as my ability to create simple graphics. Additionally, the video exhibits the time I took to learn about the how-to genre and my understanding of rhetoric. These two skills allowed me to create an informative and thorough guide to persuade audiences to follow my instruction, as well as sell Outlook as a product that is worth exploring and learning more about its other tools.

White Paper & Infographic Project

In my paper, I focus on persuading health experts and scientists on Youtube to use the communication tactics I outline when encouraging the public to follow COVID safety guidelines and have their videos better reach and resonate with audiences during the pandemic.

Creating this document required extensive research, as well as knowledge of the genres of the white paper and infographic, through spending time learning about each genre. Additionally, this project allowed me to express the understanding I've gained through personal research on how to successfully communicate on social media and my ability to write rhetorically as an English student concentrating in writing and rhetoric.

Calvert County: Past & Present Agriculture Podcast

The final presentation for the project required creating a 5-minute podcast that reflects on the content of the 1-hour interview on agriculture in Calvert County. More specifically, the audio introduces the subject of the interview, Susie Hance-Wells, and conveys the important sentiments she emphasized on the current policies and restrictions on those in the agricultural field and the importance of supporting those in the occupation. Creating the podcast required learning how to use the program Audicity as well as using what I learned in ENGH 412 to best represent the voice of my interviewee.
